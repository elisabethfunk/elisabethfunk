about elisabeth

I have a habit of stringing words together. I add magic to everything I write. (Literally. I can't make it one single chapter without inserting at least one potion or spell.) I'm excited by and anxious about technology all the same. I have strong opinions about religion and gods and What Life Means. I'm terrified of the apocalypse. Don't @ me with your Yellowstone theories.

The Golden Compass shaped me. Harry Potter stole my soul and Dragon Age's Alistair kidnapped my heart. (What do you mean, I can't make every love interest a rogueish goof?) Stardew Valley made me forget about sunlight. I'd cross a kingdom for Kaz Brekker. I've watched Moulin Rouge at least 25 times. I am 50% Mia Thermopolis.

I live in Denver, Colorado, with a man I bought on the internet, a good doggo who thinks every day is the best day for tug-of-war (especially when it's snowing), and two cats. One is my best friend and the other silently judges us all.

In my other life I'm a freelance writer and desk jockey, depending on the day.

My pen name honors my middle name (my favorite name) and my late grandmother, who was the best feminist Mennonite writer (phew, that's a mouthful, but it's all true) on the planet.

I tweet. Follow me!

about my fiction

Of Souls and Saviours is about magic, god, growing up, and kissing. It's about ghosts, literal and metaphorical. It's about best friends and losing them and finding them again in unexpected places. It's also about:

  • Memory-restoring potions
  • Radios tuned to the frequency of the dead
  • Schools built in graveyards
  • Missing singers
  • Frontier medicine shows
  • Glass-bottomed boats
  • Fancy costumes
  • Losing religion
  • First hangovers and first heartbreaks.
